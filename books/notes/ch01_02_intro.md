# Chapters 1-2: Introduction

## Who the book is for
Fundamental equity investors (portfolio managers at hedge funds) who want to use
quantitative tools to improve their process — not replace it.

## The 8 problems the book addresses

1. **Risk decomposition** — How much of my PnL is factor vs. idiosyncratic?
2. **Factor risk management** — How to reduce factor exposures without destroying alpha.
3. **Alpha sizing** — How to convert investment theses into dollar positions.
4. **Performance attribution** — Am I good at selection? Sizing? Timing?
5. **Stop-loss design** — When to cut losses and how aggressively.
6. **Leverage** — How much leverage is sustainable for my strategy?
7. **Alternative data** — How to evaluate and integrate new data sources.
8. **Portfolio construction** — The bridge between ideas and execution.

## Core philosophy
- Factor PnL is noise for a fundamental PM. Idiosyncratic PnL is where skill lives.
- "Separation of concerns" — manage factor risk separately from stock selection.
- Simple heuristics often beat sophisticated optimization in practice.
- The book is not about alpha generation — it's about everything that happens *after*
  you have an investment thesis.

## Key recurring themes
- Sharpe Ratio and Information Ratio as the primary metrics.
- Variance "adds up" (Pythagoras) — volatility does not.
- Factor models as the skeleton supporting all analysis.
- Proportional sizing beats mean-variance in practice.
- Volatility targeting beats GMV targeting.
